## 8. Definitions of Observations and Audit Outcome
● Critical: Deficiencies with established SOPs, and/or current regulatory expectations that provide  immediate and direct risk to product quality, patient safety or data integrity could potentially result  in a critical observation from a regulatory agency. Immediate corrective action and reporting to  Sponsor(s) management is required. 
● Major: Deficiencies with established SOPs, and/or current regulatory expectations that provide a  potentially significant risk to product quality, patient safety or data integrity; or could potentially  result in a major observation from a regulatory agency.

● Minor: Deficiencies of an isolated nature that are not deemed critical or major but require  correction. These may be errors that do not significantly impact GXP compliance but may have  an adverse outcome for the study or data collected. 
● Comments: Recommendations or proposals from auditor(s) which would improve effectiveness  or efficiency.  
o Increased frequency of an audit observation may raise the level of significance  (observation of trend).
